about

mun_fases (otherwise known as 'muf') is a small project where I post a daily collage including the day's lunar phase, a mirror selfie of a cutie, and other images.

concept

As far as inspirations go, I started posting these pictures in mid-2018 when trying to get in better touch with mood swings and my menstrual cycle.

I don't know that there's a physical connection between a person's hormone levels and the phases of the moon, but I do know that there is at the very least a conceptual connection there. Anyway, I was looking to get in better touch with my daily moods, so started posting these images daily.

This project is also a consideration of how people relate to astrology. I don't know *that* much about the subject, but in contemplative or confusing moments, I find myself thinking of myself and my relation to my surroundings through its scope. I have a sun and moon in sagittarius, with libra ascending. What does this mean to me? I'm not quite sure, but I think it's interesting that I find it interesting. I like how it combines structure with subjectivity. It inspires me to drink more water, it makes me think about "the elements", aka, everything solid that I might otherwise not think about.

methods

Most of non-selfie images I use are graciously donated by my boyfriend, who likes collecting things (like photographs of textures seen around). Some of them are also taken by me, and others are donated by other friends.

The majority of selfies are images that I request to use (mostly by scrolling through #mirrorselfie and #reflection tags on instagram). Some people are ok with me using their photos, some people are enthusiastically ok with it, some people don't respond (because we all get random comments on our photos sometimes, and who has time for that), some people would rather that I don't include their photos, and some people like my request without responding.

Starting out, I used Gimp to make my posts. It's a really great piece of open-source image-editing software. If you want a free alternative to photoshop, I'd recommend it.

After a month or so, I started building a web app using Django, the python image library (PIL), and Lev Pasha's python port of an unofficial instagram API in an effort to better organize the photos and automate the posts. It's helpful in terms of keeping track of which photos people have said I can use. It's still a work in progress, and pretty specified to the task at hand and probably not very useful to anyone else, but you can feel free to check out the github repo if you're interested in making a similar project.

findings and unexpected challenges

I've learned a few things during this experience.

  • My favorite part of this project is asking people if I can use their mirror selfies. No matter if they say "yes, please" or "no, thanks", it is the most interesting part of this project for me.
  • The new moon versus the full moon: how can I distinguish between the two? I have been really reluctant to post new moon images where there is no reference to a moon shape. Maybe I should take these days off, or leave the mirror selfie out of these posts.
  • Once someone has agreed to let me use their photo, I suddenly feel a sense of responsibility towards the participant. I don't want to experiment with aesthetically "unpleasing" foreground and background images when someone else's face is involved.
  • Making an image editing platform is challenging. I feel much more respect towards image editing software than I did previously. The little webapp that I use is super basic (I can't even move images around in relation to each other at this point -- they are basically stuck how I've uploaded them). Of course, having these limitations built into the platform is also a blessing, since only some foreground/background images will fit with a given mirror selfie (unless I jump through some loops and workarounds), and similarly, certain mirror selfies will only work with certain moon phases.

future

I don't know where I want to go with this project, but I will keep posting pictures until I've reached a year of posts. At that point, I'll re-evaluate and decide whether the project has reached it's natural end or if it still has life. Perhaps we will find it a new direction.

If you have any thoughts or suggestions for the future of this project, please reach out to me by dm'ing me on instagram. Please also check out how to participate.

For now, here are a couple small goals I have for mun_fases:

  • write more interesting captions
  • experiment with gradually changing the theme of the images (like these accounts which move slowly from one color palette to another over time)
  • write some daily posts about my moods (maybe unrelated to this project)
  • try something besides mirror selfies (i.e. pictures people take of their shadows... harder to find, though, and sometimes so beautiful!)
  • update this platform so that others can submit images on here / participate on other ways
